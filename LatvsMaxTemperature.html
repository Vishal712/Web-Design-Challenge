Max Temperature


Comparing Lattitude to Max Temperature in each city.

This plot shows a comparison of Lattitude and the max temperature recorded in each city. As we can see, there is a clear bell curve effect here that signals a low max temperature as lattitude is lower, which are the negative values, and the max temperature clearly gets higher as lattitude gets near 0. However, the max temperature drops once it gets to a lattitude of around 30 and beyond.
The explanation for this is probably because the temperature is approaching the equator as the lattitude is getting closer 0, and as it gets a larger number, both positive and negative, then it decreases the max temperature. An easy explanation of this is like the effect and temperature of the North and South pole.
